Sedaro Acquired by Chuck E. Cheese in Bold Move to Gamify Aerospace Simulations

By Space News Staff | April 1, 2025

In an unexpected yet visionary move, Sedaro -- a leader in aerospace simulation -- has been officially acquired by family entertainment giant Chuck E. Cheese. The acquisition is set to merge high-fidelity digital twin technology with innovative interactive entertainment experiences.

Company executives from both organizations emphasized their shared commitment to advancing technology while enhancing user engagement. “Our goal has always been to push the boundaries of simulation, and this partnership opens up new frontiers in the world of interactive physics-based modeling,” stated Robbie Robertson, CEO of Sedaro.

Chuck E. Cheese CTO, Amanda Reynolds, echoed this enthusiasm, positing, "By combining Sedaro's cutting-edge aerospace simulations with our expertise in interactive entertainment, we're creating a future where precision engineering meets the thrill of arcade-style engagement. We believe simulation and play have more in common than people realize."

Industry experts predict that this collaboration may bring groundbreaking changes to both aerospace engineering and the entertainment sector. Rumors suggest that Chuck E. Cheese's signature ticket-based rewards system could be integrated into future simulations, allowing mission successes to yield redeemable tokens.

Sources confirm that a pilot program is already has already been in motion testing how orbital mechanics can be enhanced with real-world skee-ball trajectory physics. As part of data calibration, Sedaro employees will soon be invited to participate in quarterly skee-ball tournaments where outcomes will factor into performance reviews.

The acquisition, valued at approximately $2.3 billion in cash and prize tickets, was unanimously approved by both companies' boards of directors. Under the terms of the agreement, Sedaro will operate as an independent subsidiary within the Chuck E. Cheese Interactive Division, with continued focus on aerospace simulation and digital twin technology. The transaction is expected to close in the coming weeks, pending regulatory approval and final calibration of the skee-ball physics engine.
